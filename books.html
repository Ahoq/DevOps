DevOps Reading List

Many of these readings long pre-date the creation (in 2008) of the term DevOps. But the DevOps movement does not pretend to have sprung, like Athena, fully-formed from the head of Zeus. All of the leading DevOps figures explicitly acknowledge their great debt to the many brilliant figures who laid the foundations for what we today call DevOps, which consists in the merging of many earlier streams of thought on how to develop software and manage its deployment and operations.

  • A Pattern Language, Christopher Alexander
  • Domain-Driven Design, Eric Evans
  • Implementing Lean Software Development, Mary and Tom Poppendieck, Jeff Sutherland, and Kent Beck
  • Just-in-Time for Today and Tomorrow, Taiichi Ohno and Setsuo Mito
  • Manifesto for Agile Software Development, multiple authors
  • " No Silver Bullet," Fred Brooks
  • Phoenix Project, Gene Kim
  • Software Tools in Pascal, Brian Kernighan and P.J. Plauger
  • The DevOps Handbook, Gene Kim, Patrick Debois, John Willis, Jez Humble, and John Allspaw
  • The Mythical Man-Month, Fred Brooks
  • The Timeless Way of Building, Christopher Alexander
  • The UNIX Programming Environment, Brian Kernighan and Rob Pike
